The Semiconscious State of Mind known as SLEEP

Read about the different cycles of sleep and common sleep disorders.
March, 2020

What is Sleep

  • Semi-conscious state of mind during which your breathing slows down, blood pressure decreases, muscles relax, and body temperature drops
  • Although your body relaxes, your brain stays active
    • Conscious mind shuts down, but neurons are still in a state of flux
  • Occurs in cycles lasting 1.5-2 hours each
    • Each cycle divided into REM and NREM sleep
      • REM = Rapid Eye Movement
      • NREM = Non Rapid Eye Movement

Cycles of Sleep

  • NREM Sleep
    • 1st phase of sleep cycle
      • Begins with a light sleep you can easily wake up from
      • 5-10 mins later: heart-rate slows down and you fall into deeper sleep
    • Eyes and brain are generally inactive but body may move around
      • Ex. Sleepwalking may occur during this phase
    • Time when the body repairs damaged tissues and grows
  • REM Sleep
    • Occurs after about 60 mins of NREM sleep
    • Eyes and brain are active but body is almost completely still
    • Dreams occur
    • Get longer as they recur through the night

5 Most Common Sleep Disorders

  • Insomnia
    • Description of condition
      • The term for difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep
      • 2 different types
        • Transient or Short-term Insomnia
          • Usually occurs after a stressful experience or event in your life
            • Ex. Work issues or relationship problems
          • May also occur if you have jet lag or an unusual work schedule
        • Chronic Insomnia
          • Having difficulty falling asleep for long periods of time (at least one month)
          • Experiencing drowsiness during the day
          • Developing a sleeping pattern where you have a a few nights of good sleep alternating with many nights of insomnia
        • Treatment
          • Medication
          • Cognitive behavior therapy
          • Lifestyle changes
    • Sleep Apnea
      • Description of condition
        • When your airway repeatedly becomes blocked in your sleep and you have problems breathing
        • You may begin making choking noises or snoring loudly
        • This may happen multiple times a night
      • Treatment
        • CRAP (Continuous Positive Airway Pressure) Therapy
          • Machine that uses mild air pressure to keep your airways open as you sleep
          • Machine is attached to a mask or prongs that fit in your nose
    • Narcolepsy
      • Description of condition
        • Causes you to suddenly fall asleep at any time
        • You may fall asleep uncontrollable during unusual circumstances, such as while eating
      • Treatment
        • Medication
        • Scheduled naps
    • Restless Legs Syndrome (RLS)
      • Description of condition
        • Uncontrollable urge or desire to move your legs while you are resting
        • You may experience aching, tingling, or other unpleasant feelings in your calves or sometimes in other parts of your body
      • Treatment
        • Medications
        • Behavioral therapy
    • REM Sleep Behavior Disorder
      • Description of condition
        • You act based on your dreams when you’re asleep
        • You can control movement in your muscles when sleeping
      • Treatment
        • Via medications
