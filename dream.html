Dreams

  • Most people have about 4-5 per night from 15-45 mins long
  • Take place during REM sleep

Why do we dream?

  • According to Sigmund Freud's theory of dreams, dreams represent unconscious desires, thoughts, wish fulfillment, and motivations.
  • According to J. Allan Hobson and Robert McCarley, who created the activation-synthesis model of dreaming in 1977, dreams are a product of mental processing
    • Circuits in the brain become activated during REM sleep. This causes the areas in the brain involved in emotions, sensations, and memories to create a perimeter of electrical brain impuleses.
      • The brain synthesizes and interprets this internal activity and attempts to find meaning in these signals, creating an experience we call dreams. This model shows that people create a subjective interpretation of these brain signals and their subconscious mind creates a cohesive story.
  • According to studies such as the information processing theory, sleep is a way for our minds to process and collect all the details and memories stored througout the day. Some dream experts suggest that dreams are produced by this mental processing.
    • Also known as the self-organization theory of dreaming, which explains dreaming as a side effect of neurological activity as memories are consolidated during sleep; helpful memories are made stronger, while less useful ones fade away
  • Other common purposes/theories relevant to dreams:
    • Dreams portray creativity
    • Dreams reflect on your experiences
    • Dreams stimulate emotions and stress
